Overview

Abstract

Recently, Vision-Language-Action Models (VLAs) have emerged as a prominent research frontier in embodied intelligence, sparking growing efforts to transfer generalist capabilities from the digital domains to the physical world. By leveraging the rich world knowledge embedded in large-scale foundation models, VLAs have progressed from task-specific policies into versatile and generalized intelligent robotic systems. Despite progress, the substantial computational demands of the large-scale foundation models, e.g., extensive model size and high training and inference costs, continue to pose major challenges for real-world deployment of VLAs, particularly in resource-constrained environments. In this survey, we aim to systematically review the emerging field of Efficient Vision-Language-Action Models (EVLAs). We begin by outlining the canonical architectures of VLAs and introducing a unified taxonomy of efficiency-oriented strategies along three key dimensions: (1) Efficient Model Design, including highly efficient architectures and model compression, (2) Efficient Training, encompassing both pre-training optimization and post-training adaptation, and (3) Efficient Data Collection, covering data acquisition, curation, and synthetic generation. As the first dedicated survey on efficient VLAs, this work establishes a foundational framework for the community and highlights critical pathways toward scalable, deployable, and sustainable embodied intelligent systems.
